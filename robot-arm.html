A Robot-Arm

When you own a 3D printer, making things becomes a whole lot easier. If you also know how to use CAD software so you can design your own parts, instead of the challenges of creation, your imagination suddenly becomes the limiting factor. So when I wanted to try my hand at robotics, my first thought naturally was to build a robot-arm myself. Though there is challenge in 3D printing, mostly to your patience, the main challenges lie with controlling the arm itself. For this I used an arduino connected to a laptop. Then I used python to create a UI in which I could control the position of the servo's of the arm so it could be moved. This information is sent to the arduino, which takes in the code, and puts the right voltages on the servo's so they can take their appropriate positions. It has been a fun project, but looking back I suddenly get urges to continue and for example create an inverse kinematic model, so I could more precisely control the position of the head of the robot-arm instead of the positions of the servo's.
